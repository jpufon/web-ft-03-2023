The Best era of Boxing

The 1990s was a golden era for boxing, with some of the sport's most iconic fights and legendary fighters. The decade saw the rise of heavyweight champions like Mike Tyson, Evander Holyfield, and Lennox Lewis, who dominated the division and engaged in historic battles. In addition to the heavyweights, there were also several notable fighters in the lower weight classes, such as Oscar De La Hoya, Roy Jones Jr., and Pernell Whitaker, who displayed incredible skill and athleticism in the ring

Boxing as a sport

The 90s boxing era is widely regarded as one of the most competitive and talented eras in the sport's history. Boxers of this era were known for their impressive skillset, including excellent footwork, speed, and agility. They had the ability to read their opponents' moves and anticipate their next move, making it difficult for their opponents to land punches.

The following skillset were needed to be a good boxer

  1. speed
  2. agility
  3. footwork
